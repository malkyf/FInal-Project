Kathryn Kosey

Kathryn Kosey has been working at HealthSpace Data Systems for 5 years and has been a web-based software developer for over 13 years. She started off in a single area of the company and now is in a team of Principal Engineers. Her team is not given a specific project but instead helps and supports other teams with their work. She has worked in system architecture, server and devops administration, data migrations and transformation, and some client project management.

Kathryn Kosey would advise somebody looking into this field to always be very curious and look for more ways to learn. You should try to see what areas of coding you really enjoy and strengthen them. She also says that when choosing a job, you should think about whether you would prefer a bigger company or a smaller company. In a bigger company you can learn from so many different people who have been coding for much longer yet in a smaller company you get to try so many new things and expand your knowledge and horizons. In addition, Kathryn says that hands on experience is super important and useful but a CS degree is also very helpful, (especially in very complex problems, AI and machine learning). She herself got her Bachelor’s in Fine Arts and a major in Digital arts with a minor in Computer Science. She says that it can be challenging during interviews but once you have experience you shouldn’t have to worry.

Something really interesting that Kathryn Kosey said is that it's really important to know how to google search. Some people know exactly what to type into the search bar and their results will appear and she said this is super important. She also said that it is important to be curious and want to learn new things. You don’t need to be creating something brand new all the time; sometimes you can create something that already exists just to see how it is done.

As Kathryn has always worked from home, Covid-19 did not change her day to day life. She has a meeting over video conference at the beginning of the week. There, everybody discusses what the plan is for the week and any current problems they are facing with their projects. After this meeting, she is usually working in team support, in other meetings about product and team strategy, or working on her current projects. She normally works around 40 hours a week yet she says that sometimes she has worked 50 or even 55 hours a week if she needed to. She says that some people work 40 hours and others work more, it just depends on the person and if they have any upcoming deadlines.
